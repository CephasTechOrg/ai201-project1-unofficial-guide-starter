CS 201 - Data Structures   |   Quality: 5.0   Difficulty: 4.0   |   Mar 2024
Marsh is hard but genuinely one of the best professors in the CS department. Her exams come straight from the lecture slides, not the textbook, so go to class and take notes. Midterms are curved, the final is not. If you keep up with the weekly problem sets you'll be fine, but if you fall behind two weeks in, it's almost impossible to recover. Attendance basically matters more than the readings.

CS 201 - Data Structures   |   Quality: 4.0   Difficulty: 5.0   |   Nov 2023
Fair but brutal. The amount of material she covers is no joke. Linked lists, trees, hash tables, graphs — all in one semester with weekly coding assignments graded on an autograder. You WILL learn data structures. Office hours are a lifesaver, she explains recursion better than anyone. Just don't expect an easy A.
